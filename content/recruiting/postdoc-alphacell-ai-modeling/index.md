We are recruiting a **postdoctoral researcher in AI modeling for cell biology**, based in the
[AICell Lab](/about) at KTH and SciLifeLab and embedded in SciLifeLab's
**[AlphaCell program](https://www.scilifelab.se/alpha-cell/)** — a flagship, Wallenberg-funded
effort to build the first molecular-level computational model of the human cell.

This is a computationally oriented role, grounded in biological questions. You will develop and
train large-scale AI models that learn from diverse biological data — fluorescence and electron
microscopy together with molecular measurements — to represent, predict and simulate how cells
are organized and behave. The work is highly collaborative across computational and experimental
teams, including close collaboration with Avlant Nilsson's group at Karolinska Institutet and the
Human Protein Atlas data team.

We shape the exact project scope together at the start, so this description stays high-level by
design. If it sounds like your kind of challenge, the full details and eligibility requirements
are in the KTH advertisement.

- **Reference:** PA-2026-2550
- **Application deadline:** 3 August 2026
- **Informal enquiries:** [wei.ouyang@scilifelab.se](mailto:wei.ouyang@scilifelab.se)

Apply through KTH's recruitment system using the **Apply** button above. This position is part of
the wider [Alpha Cell postdoc call](https://www.scilifelab.se/alpha-cell/), which includes further
openings across the program.
